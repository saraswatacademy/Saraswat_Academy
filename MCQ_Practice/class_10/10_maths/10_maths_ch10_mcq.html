Class 10 Mathematics

Chapter 10: Circles

50 Board-Level MCQs with Answers and Solutions

Q1. The tangent to a circle at any point is

(a) Parallel to the radius
(b) Perpendicular to the radius through the point of contact
(c) Equal to the radius
(d) A diameter

Answer: (b) Perpendicular to the radius through the point of contact

Q2. A tangent intersects a circle at

(a) Two points
(b) Three points
(c) One point
(d) No point

Answer: (c) One point

Q3. The common point of a tangent and a circle is called

(a) Centre
(b) Chord
(c) Point of Contact
(d) Radius

Answer: (c) Point of Contact

Q4. How many tangents can be drawn at a point on a circle?

(a) One
(b) Two
(c) Three
(d) Infinite

Answer: (a) One

Q5. The angle between a tangent and the radius through the point of contact is

(a) 30°
(b) 45°
(c) 60°
(d) 90°

Answer: (d) 90°

Q6. The number of tangents that can be drawn from an external point to a circle is

(a) 1
(b) 2
(c) 3
(d) Infinite

Answer: (b) 2

Q7. Tangents drawn from an external point to a circle are

(a) Unequal
(b) Perpendicular
(c) Equal in length
(d) Parallel

Answer: (c) Equal in length

Q8. If PA and PB are tangents from external point P, then

(a) PA > PB
(b) PA < PB
(c) PA = PB
(d) PA + PB = 0

Answer: (c) PA = PB

Q9. A line which cuts a circle at two distinct points is called

(a) Tangent
(b) Radius
(c) Secant
(d) Diameter

Answer: (c) Secant

Q10. The longest chord of a circle is

(a) Radius
(b) Tangent
(c) Diameter
(d) Arc

Answer: (c) Diameter

Q11. A tangent to a circle and the radius through the point of contact are

(a) Parallel
(b) Perpendicular
(c) Equal
(d) Coincident

Answer: (b)

Q12. If radius of a circle is 5 cm, then diameter is

(a) 5 cm
(b) 7 cm
(c) 10 cm
(d) 15 cm

Answer: (c) 10 cm

Q13. The point from which two tangents are drawn lies

(a) Inside the circle
(b) On the circle
(c) Outside the circle
(d) At the centre

Answer: (c)

Q14. If PA and PB are tangents from P and PA = 8 cm, then PB =

(a) 4 cm
(b) 8 cm
(c) 12 cm
(d) 16 cm

Answer: (b)

Q15. A tangent and a secant differ because

(a) Tangent touches at one point, secant cuts at two points
(b) Both touch at one point
(c) Both cut at two points
(d) Tangent passes through centre

Answer: (a)

Q16. How many tangents can be drawn from a point on the circle?

(a) 0
(b) 1
(c) 2
(d) Infinite

Answer: (b)

Q17. The centre of a circle lies on the

(a) Radius drawn to point of contact
(b) Tangent
(c) Arc
(d) Chord

Answer: (a)

Q18. If O is centre and PT is tangent at T, then ∠OTP equals

(a) 30°
(b) 45°
(c) 60°
(d) 90°

Answer: (d)

Q19. A circle can have how many tangents?

(a) One
(b) Two
(c) Four
(d) Infinite

Answer: (d)

Q20. If radius = 7 cm, diameter =

(a) 7 cm
(b) 10 cm
(c) 12 cm
(d) 14 cm

Answer: (d)

Q21. (CBSE PYQ) If two tangents are drawn from an external point P, then PA and PB are

(a) Unequal
(b) Equal
(c) Parallel
(d) Perpendicular

Answer: (b)

Q22. Which statement is true?

(a) Radius is parallel to tangent
(b) Radius is perpendicular to tangent at point of contact
(c) Radius and tangent coincide
(d) Radius bisects tangent

Answer: (b)

Q23. The distance from the centre of a circle to a tangent is equal to

(a) Diameter
(b) Radius
(c) Chord
(d) Arc

Answer: (b)

Q24. A tangent to a circle cannot pass through the

(a) Point of contact
(b) Exterior point
(c) Centre
(d) Boundary of circle

Answer: (c)

Q25. If OP = 13 cm and radius = 5 cm, then tangent length PT is

(a) 10 cm
(b) 11 cm
(c) 12 cm
(d) 13 cm

Answer: (c) 12 cm

Solution: PT² = OP² − OT² = 13² − 5² = 144.

Q26. (CBSE PYQ) The tangent at any point of a circle is perpendicular to

(a) Chord
(b) Arc
(c) Diameter
(d) Radius through that point

Answer: (d)

Q27. If two tangents are drawn from point P, then triangle formed with centre O is

(a) Equilateral
(b) Isosceles
(c) Scalene
(d) Right only

Answer: (b)

Q28. A circle and tangent have exactly

(a) No common point
(b) One common point
(c) Two common points
(d) Three common points

Answer: (b)

Q29. If radius is doubled, diameter becomes

(a) Double
(b) Triple
(c) Four times
(d) Half

Answer: (a)

Q30. The angle between two radii of a circle is called

(a) Central Angle
(b) Tangent Angle
(c) Reflex Angle
(d) Exterior Angle

Answer: (a)

Q31. If a tangent touches the circle at A, then OA is

(a) Chord
(b) Radius
(c) Diameter
(d) Secant

Answer: (b)

Q32. If PA and PB are tangents and ∠APB = 60°, then triangle APB is

(a) Isosceles
(b) Equilateral
(c) Scalene
(d) Right

Answer: (a)

Q33. The shortest distance from centre to tangent is

(a) Radius
(b) Diameter
(c) Chord
(d) Arc

Answer: (a)

Q34. Which of the following touches the circle at one point?

(a) Diameter
(b) Chord
(c) Tangent
(d) Secant

Answer: (c)

Q35. If the radius of a circle is 10 cm, then the distance from centre to tangent is

(a) 5 cm
(b) 10 cm
(c) 15 cm
(d) 20 cm

Answer: (b)

Q36. (CBSE PYQ) If OP = 17 cm and radius = 8 cm, then tangent length from P is

(a) 15 cm
(b) 16 cm
(c) 17 cm
(d) 18 cm

Answer: (a)

Solution: √(17² − 8²) = √225 = 15 cm.

Q37. The theorem "Tangents from an external point are equal" applies to

(a) One tangent
(b) Two tangents
(c) Three tangents
(d) No tangent

Answer: (b)

Q38. Which line segment joins the centre to any point on the circle?

(a) Chord
(b) Diameter
(c) Radius
(d) Tangent

Answer: (c)

Q39. A secant intersects a circle at

(a) One point
(b) Two points
(c) Three points
(d) No points

Answer: (b)

Q40. If radius is 9 cm, diameter is

(a) 9 cm
(b) 18 cm
(c) 27 cm
(d) 36 cm

Answer: (b)

Q41. A tangent can be drawn at how many points on a circle?

(a) One
(b) Two
(c) Four
(d) Every point of the circle

Answer: (d)

Q42. (CBSE PYQ) If PA and PB are tangents from P, then PA − PB equals

(a) 1
(b) 0
(c) Radius
(d) Diameter

Answer: (b)

Q43. The point where a tangent touches the circle is called

(a) Centre
(b) Chord
(c) Point of Contact
(d) Radius

Answer: (c)

Q44. If OP = 25 cm and radius = 7 cm, tangent length is

(a) 24 cm
(b) 25 cm
(c) 26 cm
(d) 27 cm

Answer: (a)

Solution: √(25² − 7²) = √576 = 24 cm.

Q45. Which theorem is used in finding tangent length from an external point?

(a) BPT
(b) Pythagoras Theorem
(c) Midpoint Theorem
(d) Similarity Theorem

Answer: (b)

Q46. The radius drawn to the point of contact is always

(a) Parallel to tangent
(b) Equal to tangent
(c) Perpendicular to tangent
(d) Longer than tangent

Answer: (c)

Q47. If radius = 12 cm, then diameter =

(a) 12 cm
(b) 18 cm
(c) 20 cm
(d) 24 cm

Answer: (d)

Q48. The tangent segment joining external point to point of contact is

(a) Radius
(b) Diameter
(c) Tangent Length
(d) Arc

Answer: (c)

Q49. If two tangents are drawn from an external point, the angle between them depends on

(a) Radius only
(b) Circle only
(c) Position of external point
(d) Diameter only

Answer: (c)

Q50. Which statement is true?

(a) A tangent cuts the circle at two points.
(b) A radius through the point of contact is perpendicular to the tangent.
(c) Tangents from an external point are unequal.
(d) A circle has only two tangents.

Answer: (b) A radius through the point of contact is perpendicular to the tangent.

Solution: This is the fundamental theorem of tangents to a circle.
