The Making of a Scientist

1. How can one become a scientist, an economist, a historian...? Does it simply involve reading many books on the subject? Does it involve observing, thinking and doing experiments?

No, one cannot become a scientist, economist, historian, or expert in any field simply by reading many books. Books provide knowledge, but true expertise requires much more than theoretical learning.

To become a scientist, a person must develop qualities such as curiosity, observation, logical thinking, patience, and a willingness to experiment. Scientists carefully observe natural phenomena, ask questions, form hypotheses, conduct experiments, and analyse results before reaching conclusions.

Similarly, an economist studies economic activities, collects data, analyses trends, and develops theories to understand how economies function. A historian examines historical records, evaluates evidence, and interprets past events to understand their significance.

Therefore, becoming an expert in any field involves a combination of reading, observing, thinking critically, asking questions, conducting investigations, and gaining practical experience. Continuous learning and dedication are essential for success.

2. You must have read about cells and DNA in your science books. Discuss Richard Ebright's work in the light of what you have studied. If you get an opportunity to work like Richard Ebright on projects and experiments, which field would you like to work on and why?

Richard Ebright's work is closely related to the study of cells, genes, and DNA. Through his experiments on butterflies, he developed a deep interest in biology and scientific research. His investigations eventually helped him understand how cells function and how genetic information controls the growth and development of living organisms.

The study of DNA is important because it contains the genetic instructions that determine the characteristics of all living things. Richard Ebright's achievements demonstrate how careful observation, experimentation, and persistence can lead to important scientific discoveries.

If I were given an opportunity to work on projects and experiments like Richard Ebright, I would choose the field of Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence. This field interests me because it combines creativity, problem-solving, mathematics, and technology. Artificial Intelligence has the potential to improve education, healthcare, agriculture, and many other areas of life. Working in this field would allow me to develop innovative solutions to real-world problems and contribute to technological advancement.

Richard Ebright's story teaches us that success in research depends on curiosity, hard work, perseverance, and a genuine love for learning.
