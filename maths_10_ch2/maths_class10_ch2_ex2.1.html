Exercise 2.1

Q1: The zeroes of a polynomial are the x-coordinates of the points where its graph cuts or touches the x-axis.

(i)

The graph is a horizontal line parallel to the x-axis and does not intersect the x-axis.

Number of zeroes = 0

(ii)

The graph cuts the x-axis at one point.

Number of zeroes = 1

(iii)

The graph cuts the x-axis at three distinct points.

Number of zeroes = 3

(iv)

The graph cuts the x-axis at two distinct points.

Number of zeroes = 2

(v)

The graph cuts the x-axis four times.

Number of zeroes = 4

(vi)

The graph intersects or touches the x-axis at four points.

  • One point on the left where the curve cuts the x-axis.
  • One point where the first valley touches the x-axis.
  • One point on the right where the curve meets the x-axis.
  • One point where the second valley touches the x-axis.

Number of zeroes = 4

Answer Table

Graph Number of Zeroes
(i) 0
(ii) 1
(iii) 3
(iv) 2
(v) 4
(vi) 4

Final Answer:
(i) 0    (ii) 1    (iii) 3    (iv) 2    (v) 4    (vi) 4
