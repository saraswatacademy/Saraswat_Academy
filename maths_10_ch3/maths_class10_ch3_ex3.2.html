EXERCISE 3.2

1. Solve the following pairs of linear equations by the substitution method

(i) Equations:

(1) x+y=14
(2) x−y=4

Step 1: From equation (2), express x in terms of y:

x=4+y --- (3)

Step 2: Substitute equation (3) into equation (1):

(4+y)+y=14

4+2y=14⇒2y=10⇒y=5

Step 3: Substitute y = 5 into equation (3):

x=4+5=9

Final Answer: x = 9, y = 5

(ii) Equations:

(1) s−t=3
(2) s3+t2=6

Step 1: From equation (1), express s in terms of t:

s=3+t --- (3)

Step 2: Substitute equation (3) into equation (2):

3+t3+t2=6

Multiply the entire equation by 6 (LCM) to clear fractions:

2(3+t)+3t=36

6+2t+3t=36⇒5t=30⇒t=6

Step 3: Substitute t = 6 into equation (3):

s=3+6=9

Final Answer: s = 9, t = 6

(iii) Equations:

(1) 3x−y=3
(2) 9x−3y=9

Step 1: From equation (1), express y in terms of x:

y=3x−3 --- (3)

Step 2: Substitute equation (3) into equation (2):

9x−3(3x−3)=9

9x−9x+9=9⇒9=9

Conclusion: Since this statement is always true, the system has infinitely many solutions.

Final Answer: Infinitely many solutions, where y = 3x - 3

(iv) Equations:

(1) 0.2x+0.3y=1.3
(2) 0.4x+0.5y=2.3

Multiply both equations by 10 to clear decimals:

(1a) 2x+3y=13
(2a) 4x+5y=23

Step 1: From equation (1a), express x in terms of y:

2x=13−3y⇒x=13−3y2 --- (3)

Step 2: Substitute equation (3) into equation (2a):

4(13−3y2)+5y=23

2(13−3y)+5y< =="23">

26−6y+5y=23⇒26−y=23⇒y=3

Step 3: Substitute y = 3 into equation (3):

x=13−3(3)2=13−92=2

Final Answer: x = 2, y = 3

(v) Equations:

(1) 2x+3y=0
(2) 3x−8y=0

Step 1: From equation (1), express x in terms of y:

2x=−3y⇒x=−32y --- (3)

Step 2: Substitute equation (3) into equation (2):

3(−32y)−8y=0

−32y−8y=0

Factor out y:

y(−32−8)=0⇒y=0

Step 3: Substitute y = 0 into equation (3):

x=−32(0)=0

Final Answer: x = 0, y = 0

(vi) Equations:

(1) 3x2−5y3=−2
(2) x3+y2=136

Multiply equation (1) by 6 and equation (2) by 6 to clear fractions:

(1a) 9x−10y=−12
(2a) 2x+3y=13

Step 1: From equation (2a), express x in terms of y:

2x=13−3y⇒x=13−3y2 --- (3)

Step 2: Substitute equation (3) into equation (1a):

9(13−3y2)−10y=−12

Multiply the entire equation by 2 to clear the fraction:

9(13−3y)−20y=−24

117−27y−20y=−24

117−47y=−24⇒−47y=−141⇒y=3

Step 3: Substitute y = 3 into equation (3):

x=13−3(3)2=13−92=2

Final Answer: x = 2, y = 3

Question 2: Solve 2x + 3y = 11 and 2x – 4y = – 24 and hence find the value of ‘m’ for which y = mx + 3.

2x + 3y = 11

2x − 4y = −24

Subtracting the second equation from the first:

(2x + 3y) − (2x − 4y) = 11 − (−24)

7y = 35

y = 5

Substituting y = 5 in 2x + 3y = 11:

2x + 15 = 11

2x = −4

x = −2

Given:

y = mx + 3

Substituting x = −2 and y = 5:

5 = m(−2) + 3

2 = −2m

m = −1

Answer: x = −2, y = 5 and m = −1.

Question 3

(i) The difference between two numbers is 26 and one number is three times the other.

Let the numbers be x and y.

x − y = 26

x = 3y

Substituting x = 3y:

3y − y = 26

2y = 26

y = 13

x = 39

Answer: The numbers are 39 and 13.

(ii) The larger of two supplementary angles exceeds the smaller by 18°.

Let the larger angle be x° and the smaller angle be y°.

x + y = 180°

x − y = 18°

From x − y = 18:

x = y + 18

Substituting:

y + 18 + y = 180

2y = 162

y = 81°

x = 99°

Answer: The angles are 99° and 81°.

(iii) Cost of Bats and Balls

Let the cost of one bat be ₹x and the cost of one ball be ₹y.

7x + 6y = 3800

3x + 5y = 1750

From:

3x + 5y = 1750

3x = 1750 − 5y

x = (1750 − 5y)/3

Substituting in 7x + 6y = 3800:

7(1750 − 5y)/3 + 6y = 3800

12250 − 35y + 18y = 11400

12250 − 17y = 11400

17y = 850

y = 50

Substituting in 3x + 5y = 1750:

3x + 250 = 1750

3x = 1500

x = 500

Answer:

  • Cost of one bat = ₹500
  • Cost of one ball = ₹50

(iv) Taxi Charges

Let the fixed charge be ₹x and the charge per kilometre be ₹y.

x + 10y = 105

x + 15y = 155

From the first equation:

x = 105 − 10y

Substituting:

105 − 10y + 15y = 155

5y = 50

y = 10

x = 105 − 100

x = 5

For 25 km:

Fare = 5 + (25 × 10)

= 255

Answer:

  • Fixed charge = ₹5
  • Charge per km = ₹10
  • Fare for 25 km = ₹255

(v) Finding the Fraction

Let the fraction be x/y.

Given:

(x + 2)/(y + 2) = 9/11

11(x + 2) = 9(y + 2)

11x − 9y = −4     (1)

Also:

(x + 3)/(y + 3) = 5/6

6(x + 3) = 5(y + 3)

6x − 5y = −3     (2)

From (2):

x = (5y − 3)/6

Substituting in (1):

11(5y − 3)/6 − 9y = −4

55y − 33 − 54y = −24

y = 9

x = 7

Answer: The fraction is 7⁄9.

(vi) Ages of Jacob and His Son

Let Jacob's present age be x years and his son's present age be y years.

Five years hence:

x + 5 = 3(y + 5)

x − 3y = 10     (1)

Five years ago:

x − 5 = 7(y − 5)

x − 7y = −30     (2)

From (1):

x = 3y + 10

Substituting in (2):

3y + 10 − 7y = −30

−4y = −40

y = 10

x = 40

Answer:

  • Jacob's present age = 40 years
  • Son's present age = 10 years
