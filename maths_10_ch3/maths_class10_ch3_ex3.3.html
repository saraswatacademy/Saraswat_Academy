EXERCISE 3.3

1. Solve the following pair of linear equations by the elimination method and the substitution method:

(i)

(1) x+y=5
(2) 2x−3y=4

Elimination Method:

Multiply equation (1) by 3:

3x+3y=15 --- (3)

Add equation (2) and equation (3):

(2x−3y)+(3x+3y)=4+15

5x=19⇒x=195

Substitute the value of x into equation (1):

195+y=5⇒y=5−195⇒y=65

Substitution Method:

From equation (1), express x in terms of y:

x=5−y --- (4)

Substitute equation (4) into equation (2):

2(5−y)−3y=4

10−2y−3y=4⇒10−5y=4⇒−5y=−6⇒y=65

Substitute the value of y into equation (4):

x=5−65=195

Final Answer: x=195, y=65

(ii)

(1) 3x+4y=10
(2) 2x−2y=2

Elimination Method:

Multiply equation (2) by 2:

4x−4y=4 --- (3)

Add equation (1) and equation (3):

(3x+4y)+(4x−4y)=10+4

7x=14⇒x=2

Substitute x = 2 into equation (2):

2(2)−2y=2⇒4−2y=2⇒−2y=−2⇒y=1

Final Answer: x = 2, y = 1

(iii)

Rearranging the given equations into standard form:
(1) 3x−5y=4
(2) 9x−2y=7

Elimination Method:

Multiply equation (1) by 3:

9x−15y=12 --- (3)

Subtract equation (3) from equation (2):

(9x−2y)−(9x−15y)=7−12

13y=−5⇒y=−513

Substitute the value of y into equation (1):

3x−5(−513)=4⇒3x+2513=4

3x=4−2513⇒3x=2713⇒x=913

Final Answer: x=913, y=−513

(iv)

Multiply first equation by 6: 3x+4y=−6 --- (1)
Multiply second equation by 3: 3x−y=9 --- (2)

Elimination Method:

Subtract equation (2) from equation (1):

(3x+4y)−(3x−y)=−6−9

Final Answer: x = 2, y = -3

Q2. Form the pair of linear equations in the following problems, and find their solutions (if they exist) by the elimination method:

(i)

Let the numerator be x and the denominator be y. The fraction is xy.

According to the given conditions:

x+1y−1=1⇒x+1=y−1⇒x−y=−2 --- (1)
xy+1=12⇒2x=y+1⇒2x−y=1 --- (2)

Subtract equation (1) from equation (2):

(2x−y)−(x−y)=1−(−2)

x=3

Substitute x = 3 into equation (1):

3−y=−2⇒−y=−5⇒y=5

Final Answer: The fraction is 35

(ii)

Let Nuri's current age be x and Sonu's current age be y.

Five years ago:

x−5=3(y−5)⇒x−5=3y−15⇒x−3y=−10 --- (1)

Ten years later:

x+10=2(y+10)⇒x+10=2y+20⇒x−2y=10 --- (2)

Subtract equation (1) from equation (2):

(x−2y)−(x−3y)=10−(−10)

y=20

Substitute y = 20 into equation (2):

x−2(20)=10⇒x−40=10⇒x=50

Final Answer: Nuri is 50 years old and Sonu is 20 years old.

(iii): The sum of the digits of a two-digit number is 9. Also, nine times this number is twice the number obtained by reversing the order of the digits. Find the number.

Solution:

Let the digit at tens place be x and the digit at units place be y.

Therefore, the original number is 10x + y.

According to the first condition, the sum of the digits is 9:

x + y = 9     — (1)

When the digits are reversed, the new number becomes 10y + x.

According to the second condition, nine times the original number is equal to twice the reversed number:

9(10x + y) = 2(10y + x)

90x + 9y = 20y + 2x

90x − 2x = 20y − 9y

88x = 11y

8x = y     — (2)

Substituting the value of y from equation (2) into equation (1):

x + 8x = 9

9x = 9

x = 1

Substituting x = 1 into equation (2):

y = 8(1) = 8

Hence, the required original number is 10(1) + 8 = 18.

(iv): Meena went to a bank to withdraw ₹ 2000. She asked the cashier to give her ₹ 50 and ₹ 100 notes only. Meena got 25 notes in all. Find how many notes of ₹ 50 and ₹ 100 she received.

Solution:

Let the number of ₹ 50 notes received be x and the number of ₹ 100 notes received be y.

Since the total number of notes received is 25, we form our first linear equation:

x + y = 25     — (1)

The total value of the withdrawn money is ₹ 2000. Therefore, the monetary sum of the notes yields:

50x + 100y = 2000

Dividing the entire equation by 50 to simplify the terms, we get:

x + 2y = 40     — (2)

Subtracting equation (1) from equation (2) to eliminate x:

(x + 2y) − (x + y) = 40 − 25

y = 15

Substituting the value of y into equation (1):

x + 15 = 25

x = 25 − 15

x = 10

Hence, Meena received 10 notes of ₹ 50 and 15 notes of ₹ 100.

(v): A lending library has a fixed charge for the first three days and an additional charge for each day thereafter. Saritha paid ₹ 27 for a book kept for seven days, while Susy paid ₹ 21 for the book she kept for five days. Find the fixed charge and the charge for each extra day.

Solution:

Let the fixed charge for the first three days be ₹ x and the additional charge for each extra day thereafter be ₹ y.

Saritha kept the book for 7 days, which means she paid the fixed charge for the first 3 days and the additional daily charge for 4 extra days (7 − 3 = 4):

x + 4y = 27     — (1)

Susy kept the book for 5 days, which means she paid the fixed charge for the first 3 days and the additional daily charge for 2 extra days (5 − 3 = 2):

x + 2y = 21     — (2)

Subtracting equation (2) from equation (1) to eliminate x:

(x + 4y) − (x + 2y) = 27 − 21

2y = 6

y = 3

Substituting the value of y into equation (2):

x + 2(3) = 21

x + 6 = 21

x = 21 − 6

x = 15

Hence, the fixed charge for the first three days is ₹ 15 and the additional charge per extra day is ₹ 3.
