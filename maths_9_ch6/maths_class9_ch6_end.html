Question 1

Identities in algebra can sometimes be shown as area relationships. For example, the figure shown corresponds to the identity (a + b)2 = a2 + 2ab + b2. Draw figures corresponding to the identities:

(i) (a + b)(a − b) = a2 − b2

(ii) (a + b + c)2 = a2 + b2 + c2 + 2ab + 2bc + 2ca

Solution:

(i) Draw a rectangle of sides (a + b) and (a − b). Divide it into regions of areas a2 and b2 to obtain:

(a + b)(a − b) = a2 − b2

(ii) Draw a square of side (a + b + c) and divide it into squares a2, b2, c2 and rectangles ab, ab, bc, bc, ca, ca.

This gives:

(a + b + c)2 = a2 + b2 + c2 + 2ab + 2bc + 2ca

Question 2

An isosceles triangle has perimeter 40 cm; the equal sides are 15 cm each. Find the area of the triangle.

Solution:

Base = 40 − 15 − 15 = 10 cm

Height = √(152 − 52)

= √(225 − 25)

= √200 = 10√2 cm

Area = 1/2 × 10 × 10√2

= 50√2 cm2

≈ 70.71 cm2

Answer: 50√2 cm2 (≈ 70.71 cm2)

Question 3

An isosceles triangle has base 10 cm, and its area is 60 cm2. What are the lengths of the equal sides?

Solution:

Area = 1/2 × Base × Height

60 = 1/2 × 10 × Height

Height = 12 cm

Half base = 5 cm

Equal side = √(122 + 52)

= √169

= 13 cm

Answer: Each equal side = 13 cm

Question 4

The area of a right-angled triangle is 54 cm2. One of its legs has length 12 cm. Find its perimeter.

Solution:

Area = 1/2 × Base × Height

54 = 1/2 × 12 × Height

Height = 9 cm

Hypotenuse = √(122 + 92)

= √225

= 15 cm

Perimeter = 12 + 9 + 15

= 36 cm

Answer: 36 cm

Question 5

The sides of a triangle are in the ratio 2 : 3 : 4, and its perimeter is 45 cm. Find its area.

Solution:

Let sides be 2x, 3x and 4x.

2x + 3x + 4x = 45

9x = 45

x = 5

Sides are 10 cm, 15 cm and 20 cm.

Semi-perimeter (s) = 45/2 = 22.5 cm

Area = √[s(s−a)(s−b)(s−c)]

= √[22.5 × 12.5 × 7.5 × 2.5]

= √5273.4375

≈ 72.62 cm2

Answer: 72.62 cm2

Question 6

The sides of a triangle have lengths 7 cm, 24 cm and 25 cm. Find the area of the triangle in two different ways.

Solution:

Since

72 + 242 = 49 + 576 = 625 = 252

The triangle is right-angled.

Method 1:

Area = 1/2 × 7 × 24

= 84 cm2

Method 2 (Heron's Formula):

s = (7 + 24 + 25)/2 = 28

Area = √[28 × 21 × 4 × 3]

= √7056

= 84 cm2

Answer: 84 cm2

Question 7

If the wheel of a bicycle has a diameter of 60 cm, find how far a cyclist will have travelled after the wheel has rotated 100 times.

Solution:

Diameter = 60 cm

Circumference = πd

= (22/7) × 60

= 1320/7 cm

Distance in 100 rotations

= 100 × 1320/7

= 132000/7 cm

= 18857.14 cm

= 188.57 m

Answer: 188.57 m

Question 8

Find the area of a quadrant of a circle whose circumference is 66 cm.

Solution:

Circumference = 66 cm

2πr = 66

2 × (22/7) × r = 66

r = 10.5 cm

Area of quadrant

= 1/4 × (22/7) × 10.5 × 10.5

= 86.625 cm2

Answer: 86.625 cm2

Question 9

The wheel of a car has an outer radius of 28 cm. Calculate how far the car travels after one complete turn of the wheel, and how many times the wheel turns during a journey of 1 km.

Solution:

Radius = 28 cm

Diameter = 56 cm

Distance travelled in one turn

= Circumference

= πd

= (22/7) × 56

= 176 cm

= 1.76 m

Distance travelled = 1 km

= 100000 cm

Number of turns

= 100000/176

= 568.18

≈ 568 turns

Answers:

Distance in one turn = 176 cm (1.76 m)

Number of turns in 1 km ≈ 568

Question 10

Two rectangles have the same area and the same perimeter. Does this mean that they are congruent to each other?

Solution:

Let the sides of the two rectangles be:

Rectangle 1 : l1, b1

Rectangle 2 : l2, b2

Given:

l1b1 = l2b2

and

2(l1 + b1) = 2(l2 + b2)

Therefore,

l1 + b1 = l2 + b2

The length and breadth of both rectangles are determined uniquely by their sum and product. Hence the corresponding sides are equal.

Therefore the rectangles are congruent.

Answer: Yes, the two rectangles are congruent.

Question 11

You know that the area of a parallelogram is base × height. Using this and the figure, show that the area of a trapezium is half the sum of the parallel sides × height, i.e.,

Area = 1/2(a + b)h

Proof:

Take two identical trapeziums and place them together to form a parallelogram.

Base of the parallelogram = a + b

Height = h

Area of parallelogram = (a + b)h

Since the parallelogram consists of two identical trapeziums,

Area of one trapezium

= 1/2(a + b)h

Hence Proved.

Question 12

By dividing a trapezium into two triangles show that its area is half the sum of the parallel sides multiplied by the height.

Proof:

Let the parallel sides be a and b and height be h.

Draw a diagonal dividing the trapezium into two triangles.

Area of first triangle = 1/2ah

Area of second triangle = 1/2bh

Total area

= 1/2ah + 1/2bh

= 1/2(a+b)h

Hence Proved.

Question 13

Show how we can use two identical copies of a trapezium to make a parallelogram. How will this give us the formula for the area of a trapezium?

Solution:

Take two identical trapeziums and place one upside down beside the other.

They form a parallelogram.

Base of parallelogram = a + b

Height = h

Area of parallelogram = (a+b)h

Since it consists of two identical trapeziums,

Area of trapezium = 1/2(a+b)h

Hence Proved.

Question 14

Show that the area of a kite is half the product of its diagonals. Show this: (i) using algebra, and (ii) using geometry.

Solution:

Let diagonals be d1 and d2.

The diagonals divide the kite into four right triangles.

Adding the areas of all four triangles gives:

Area of kite = 1/2d1d2

Thus,

Area of kite = 1/2 × (Product of diagonals)

Hence Proved.

Question 15 (i)

Rectangle ABCD has sides a, b and rectangle PQRS has sides 2a, 2b. Show that PQRS has 4 times the area of ABCD. Does this mean that 4 copies of rectangle ABCD will fit into rectangle PQRS?

Solution:

Area of ABCD = ab

Area of PQRS = (2a)(2b)

= 4ab

= 4 × Area of ABCD

Yes. Four copies of rectangle ABCD fit exactly into rectangle PQRS.

Answer: Area ratio = 4 : 1

Question 15 (ii)

ΔABC has sides a, b, c and ΔPQR has sides 2a, 2b, 2c. Show that ΔPQR has 4 times the area of ΔABC. Does this mean that 4 copies of ΔABC will fit into ΔPQR?

Solution:

All sides are doubled.

For similar triangles, area ratio equals square of scale factor.

Area ratio = 22 = 4

Therefore,

Area(ΔPQR) = 4 × Area(ΔABC)

Yes. Four copies of ΔABC fit exactly into ΔPQR.

Answer: Area ratio = 4 : 1

Question 15 (iii)

ΔABC has sides a, b, c and ΔPQR has sides 3a, 3b, 3c. Show that ΔPQR has 9 times the area of ΔABC. Does this mean that 9 copies of ΔABC will fit into ΔPQR?

Solution:

Scale factor = 3

Area ratio = 32 = 9

Therefore,

Area(ΔPQR) = 9 × Area(ΔABC)

Yes. Nine copies of ΔABC fit exactly into ΔPQR.

Answer: Area ratio = 9 : 1

Question 19

The figure shows nine identical rectangles fitted together to make a large rectangle whose area is 72 cm2. Find the perimeter of each small rectangle.

Solution:

Let the length of each small rectangle be l cm and breadth be b cm.

From the arrangement:

4b = 5l

Therefore,

l = 4x and b = 5x

Area of large rectangle = 72 cm2

Since there are 9 identical rectangles,

Area of one rectangle = 72/9 = 8 cm2

Therefore,

lb = 8

(4x)(5x) = 8

20x2 = 8

x2 = 2/5

x = √(2/5)

Perimeter of one rectangle

= 2(l+b)

= 2(4x+5x)

= 18x

= 18√(2/5)

≈ 11.38 cm

Answer: 11.38 cm
