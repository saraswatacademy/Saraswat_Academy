Q1. The perimeter of a circle is 44 cm. What is its radius?

Given: Circumference = 44 cm

Formula:

C = 2πr

44 = 2 × 22/7 × r

44 = 44/7 r

r = 44 ÷ (44/7)

r = 44 × 7/44

r = 7 cm

Answer: Radius = 7 cm

Q3 Calculate the length of the arc of a circle if: (i) the radius is 3.5 cm and the angle at the centre is 60°, and (ii) the radius is 6.3 m and the angle at the centre is 120°.

Arc Length = (60/360) × 2πr

= (60/360) × 2 × 22/7 × 3.5

= 1/6 × 22

= 11/3

= 3.67 cm

Answer: 3.67 cm

Q3 (ii). Radius = 6.3 m, Angle = 120°

Arc Length = (120/360) × 2 × 22/7 × 6.3

= 1/3 × 39.6

= 13.2 m

Answer: 13.2 m

Q4. Find the perimeter of a sector (i.e., the curved portion as well as the two straight portions) of a circle of radius 14 cm and sector angle 75°.

Arc Length = (75/360) × 2 × 22/7 × 14

= (75/360) × 88

= 55/3

= 18.33 cm

Perimeter of Sector = Arc Length + 2r

= 18.33 + 28

= 46.33 cm

Answer: 46.33 cm

Question 6

The diameter of a car tyre is 56 cm, then:

(i) How far does the car need to travel for the tyre to complete one revolution?

(ii) How many revolutions does the tyre make if the car travels 10 km?

Solution:

Diameter = 56 cm

π = 22/7

(i) Distance travelled in one revolution

Circumference = πd

= 22/7 × 56

= 176 cm

Answer: The tyre travels 176 cm in one revolution.

(ii) Number of revolutions in 10 km

10 km = 10 × 1000 × 100

= 10,00,000 cm

Number of revolutions = Total distance ÷ Circumference

= 10,00,000 ÷ 176

= 5681.82

≈ 5682 revolutions

Answer: The tyre makes approximately 5682 revolutions.

Question 7

Find the total perimeter of all the petals in each of the given flowers.

(i)

Side of square = 14 cm

Radius of each arc = 7 cm

Each petal consists of two quarter-circles.

There are 4 petals.

Total perimeter of all petals

= 8 × (Quarter-circle arc)

= 8 × 1/4 × 2πr

= 4πr

= 4 × 22/7 × 7

= 88 cm

Answer: 88 cm

(ii)

Side of regular hexagon = 42 cm

Radius of each arc = 42 cm

Each petal consists of two arcs of 60°

There are 6 petals.

Total perimeter of all petals

= 12 × (60/360) × 2π × 42

= 12 × 1/6 × 2π × 42

= 4π × 42

= 168π

= 168 × 22/7

= 528 cm

Answer: 528 cm

Question 8

The ratio of the perimeters of two circles is 5 : 4. What is the ratio of their radii?

Solution:

Circumference of a circle = 2πr

Since circumference is directly proportional to radius,

C1 : C2 = r1 : r2

5 : 4 = r1 : r2

Answer: 5 : 4
