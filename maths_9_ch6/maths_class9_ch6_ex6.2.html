Question 1

Find the area of triangle ADE in Fig. 6.31.

Length of rectangle = 10 cm

Breadth of rectangle = AD = 8 cm

Area of ΔADE = 1/2 × Base × Height

= 1/2 × AD × Distance of E from AD

= 1/2 × 8 × 10

= 40 cm2

Answer: 40 cm2

Question 2

The parallel sides of a trapezium are 40 cm and 20 cm. If its non-parallel sides are both equal, each being 26 cm, find the area of the trapezium.

Difference of parallel sides = 40 − 20 = 20 cm

Half difference = 10 cm

Height = √(262 − 102)

= √(676 − 100)

= √576

= 24 cm

Area of trapezium = 1/2 × (Sum of parallel sides) × Height

= 1/2 × (40 + 20) × 24

= 720 cm2

Answer: 720 cm2

Question 3

Find the area of a triangle, given that its sides are 8 cm and 11 cm long, and its perimeter is 32 cm.

Third side = 32 − (8 + 11)

= 13 cm

Semi-perimeter (s) = 32/2 = 16 cm

By Heron's Formula:

Area = √[s(s−a)(s−b)(s−c)]

= √[16 × 8 × 5 × 3]

= √1920

= 8√30 cm2

≈ 43.82 cm2

Answer: 8√30 cm2 (≈ 43.82 cm2)

Question 4

The sides of a triangular plot are in the ratio 3 : 5 : 7; its perimeter is 300 m. Find its area.

Total ratio = 3 + 5 + 7 = 15

One part = 300/15 = 20 m

Sides = 60 m, 100 m, 140 m

Semi-perimeter (s) = 150 m

Area = √[150 × (150−60) × (150−100) × (150−140)]

= √(150 × 90 × 50 × 10)

= √6750000

= 1500√3 m2

≈ 2598.08 m2

Answer: 1500√3 m2 (≈ 2598.08 m2)

Question 5

One diagonal of a rhombus is twice as long as the other diagonal. If the rhombus has area 128 cm2, find the length of the shorter diagonal.

Let shorter diagonal = x cm

Then longer diagonal = 2x cm

Area of rhombus = 1/2 × d1 × d2

128 = 1/2 × x × 2x

128 = x2

x = √128

x = 8√2 cm

≈ 11.31 cm

Answer: 8√2 cm (≈ 11.31 cm)

Question 6

ABCD is a parallelogram. P and Q are any two points on side AB. What can you say about the ratio area (ΔPCD) : area (ΔQCD)?

Triangles PCD and QCD have the same base CD.

Since AB || CD, the perpendicular distance of P and Q from CD is the same.

Therefore, triangles PCD and QCD have the same base and the same height.

Hence,

Area (ΔPCD) = Area (ΔQCD)

Therefore,

Area (ΔPCD) : Area (ΔQCD) = 1 : 1

Answer: 1 : 1

Question 7

O is any point on the diagonal PR of a parallelogram PQRS. Prove that the areas of triangles PSO and PQO are equal.

In parallelogram PQRS,

PS = QR and PQ = SR

Diagonal PR divides the parallelogram into two triangles of equal area.

Point O lies on diagonal PR.

Triangles PSO and PQO have the same altitude from S and Q respectively to PR.

Therefore,

Area (ΔPSO) = Area (ΔPQO)

Answer: Area (ΔPSO) = Area (ΔPQO)

Question 8

If the mid-points of the sides of a quadrilateral are joined in order, prove that the area of the parallelogram thus formed is half of the area of the quadrilateral.

Joining the mid-points of the sides of any quadrilateral forms a parallelogram (Varignon's Theorem).

The area of this parallelogram is always equal to one-half of the area of the original quadrilateral.

Therefore,

Area of formed parallelogram = 1/2 × Area of quadrilateral

Answer: The area of the parallelogram formed is half the area of the given quadrilateral.

Question 9

In ΔABC, D is the midpoint of BC. Median AD is drawn. P is any point on AD. Show that area (ΔABP) = area (ΔACP).

Since D is the midpoint of BC,

BD = DC

Therefore, AD is a median.

A median divides a triangle into two equal-area triangles.

Hence,

Area (ΔABD) = Area (ΔACD)

Point P lies on AD.

Thus triangles ABP and ACP have equal heights from B and C on AD and equal corresponding bases.

Therefore,

Area (ΔABP) = Area (ΔACP)

Answer: Area (ΔABP) = Area (ΔACP)

Question 10

Given a square ABCD, let P be a point within it. Join PA, PB, PC and PD. What is the ratio of the areas of the red region (ΔPAB and ΔPCD) and the green region (ΔPBC and ΔPDA)?

Area of square ABCD = Area(ΔPAB) + Area(ΔPBC) + Area(ΔPCD) + Area(ΔPDA)

Since opposite sides of a square are parallel,

Area(ΔPAB) + Area(ΔPCD) = Area(ΔPBC) + Area(ΔPDA)

Therefore,

Red Region : Green Region = 1 : 1

Answer: 1 : 1

Question 11

In ΔABC, D is the midpoint of BC. P is any point on BC and Q is a point on AB such that CQ || PD. PQ is joined. Prove that Area(ΔBPQ) = 1/2 Area(ΔABC).

Since D is the midpoint of BC,

BD = DC

Also,

CQ || PD

Using properties of similar triangles formed by parallel lines, the corresponding heights are proportional.

Hence triangle BPQ occupies exactly half the area of triangle ABC.

Therefore,

Area(ΔBPQ) = 1/2 Area(ΔABC)

Answer: Area(ΔBPQ) = 1/2 Area(ΔABC)
