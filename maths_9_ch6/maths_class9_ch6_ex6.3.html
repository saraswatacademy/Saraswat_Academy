Class 9 - Chapter 6: Measuring Space: Perimeter and Area

Circle Solutions

Areas Related to Circles

Unless stated otherwise, use π = 22/7.

Question 1

Find the area of a sector of a circle with radius 7 cm if the angle of the sector is 60°.

Solution:

Radius (r) = 7 cm

Angle of sector (θ) = 60°

Area of sector = (θ/360) × πr2

= (60/360) × (22/7) × 7 × 7

= (1/6) × 154

= 77/3

= 25.67 cm2

Answer: 25.67 cm2

Question 2

Find the area of a quadrant of a circle whose circumference is 44 cm.

Solution:

Circumference = 44 cm

2πr = 44

2 × (22/7) × r = 44

r = 7 cm

Area of quadrant = (1/4) × πr2

= (1/4) × (22/7) × 7 × 7

= 154/4

= 38.5 cm2

Answer: 38.5 cm2

Question 3

The length of the minute hand of a clock is 7 cm. Find the area swept by the minute hand in 10 minutes.

Solution:

Radius = 7 cm

In 60 minutes, the minute hand sweeps 360°.

In 10 minutes, angle swept = (10/60) × 360°

= 60°

Area swept = (60/360) × (22/7) × 7 × 7

= (1/6) × 154

= 77/3

= 25.67 cm2

Answer: 25.67 cm2

Question 4

A chord of a circle of radius 10 cm subtends 90° at the centre. Find the area of the corresponding:

(i) Minor sector (that subtends 90° at the centre)

(ii) Major sector (that subtends 270° at the centre)

Use π = 3.14

Solution:

Radius = 10 cm

(i) Area of Minor Sector

= (90/360) × 3.14 × 102

= (1/4) × 314

= 78.5 cm2

Answer: 78.5 cm2

(ii) Area of Major Sector

= (270/360) × 3.14 × 102

= (3/4) × 314

= 235.5 cm2

Answer: 235.5 cm2

Question 5

A chord of a circle of radius 15 cm subtends an angle of 60° at the centre. Find the areas of the corresponding minor and major segments of the circle.

Use π = 3.14 and √3 = 1.73.

Solution:

Radius = 15 cm

Angle = 60°

Area of sector = (60/360) × 3.14 × 15 × 15

= 117.75 cm2

Area of equilateral triangle

= (√3/4) × 152

= (1.73/4) × 225

= 97.31 cm2

Minor Segment

= Area of sector − Area of triangle

= 117.75 − 97.31

= 20.44 cm2

Major Segment

Area of circle = 3.14 × 15 × 15

= 706.5 cm2

= 706.5 − 20.44

= 686.06 cm2

Answers:

Minor Segment = 20.44 cm2

Major Segment = 686.06 cm2

Question 6

A car has two wipers which do not overlap. Each wiper has a blade of length 28 cm and sweeps through an angle of 120°. Find the total area cleaned at each sweep of the blades.

Solution:

Radius = 28 cm

Angle swept = 120°

Area cleaned by one wiper

= (120/360) × (22/7) × 28 × 28

= (1/3) × 2464

= 821.33 cm2

Area cleaned by two wipers

= 2 × 821.33

= 1642.67 cm2

Answer: 1642.67 cm2

Question 7

A chord of a circle of radius r subtends an angle of 60° at the centre of the circle. Show that the area of the corresponding minor segment of the circle is equal to r2(π/6 − √3/4).

Proof:

Area of minor segment

= Area of sector − Area of triangle

Area of 60° sector = (60/360)πr2

= πr2/6

Area of equilateral triangle of side r

= (√3/4)r2

Therefore,

Area of minor segment

= πr2/6 − (√3/4)r2

= r2(π/6 − √3/4)

Hence Proved.

Question 8

An equilateral triangle is inscribed in a circle of radius r. Show that the ratio of the area of the triangle to the area of the circle is equal to 3√3/(4π) ≈ 0.413.

Proof:

For an equilateral triangle inscribed in a circle,

Side = √3 r

Area of triangle

= (√3/4)(√3r)2

= (3√3/4)r2

Area of circle = πr2

Required ratio

= [(3√3/4)r2] / [πr2]

= 3√3/(4π)

≈ 0.413

Hence Proved.

Question 9

A square is inscribed in a circle of radius r. Show that the ratio of the area of the square to the area of the circle is equal to 2/π ≈ 0.637.

Proof:

Diagonal of square = Diameter of circle = 2r

Side = 2r/√2

= r√2

Area of square

= (r√2)2

= 2r2

Area of circle = πr2

Required ratio

= 2r2/πr2

= 2/π

≈ 0.637

Hence Proved.

Question 10

A hexagon is inscribed in a circle of radius r. Show that the ratio of the area of the hexagon to the area of the circle is equal to 3√3/(2π) ≈ 0.827. Can you see why the answer is exactly twice the answer to Question 8?

Proof:

A regular hexagon consists of 6 equilateral triangles of side r.

Area of one equilateral triangle

= (√3/4)r2

Area of hexagon

= 6 × (√3/4)r2

= (3√3/2)r2

Area of circle = πr2

Required ratio

= [(3√3/2)r2] / [πr2]

= 3√3/(2π)

≈ 0.827

Also,

3√3/(2π) = 2 × [3√3/(4π)]

Therefore, the answer is exactly twice the answer obtained in Question 8.

Hence Proved.
